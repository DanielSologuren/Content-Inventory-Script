Food Security Measurement

USDA has worked with the research community through the Food Security Measurement Project to develop a rigorous measure of household food security. This methodology empowers local communities to assess their own food security through the Guide described above.  It has also been incorporated into the national nutrition monitoring system, providing a rich data source for analyzing changes in the level and distribution of food security in the United States.

Food Insecurity and Hunger in the United States: An Assessment of the Measure The Committee on National Statistics of the National Academies convened a panel of experts at USDA's request to review the concepts and methods for measuring food security and hunger. This final report presents the expert panel's major findings, conclusions, and recommendations.

Entire Report

Household Food Security in the United States, 2008 The latest in a series of annual statistical reports on the prevalence of food insecurity in U.S. households, based on the December 2008 Current Population Survey Food Security Supplement. This report, in addition to statistics on food security, includes information on how much U.S. households spend on food and the extent to which food-insecure households participated in Federal and community food assistance programs.
